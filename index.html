7. Summary & Recommendation

Bottom line (full season data): With the complete data window, Option 1 meaningfully outperforms Option 2 on both appointment handling (37.2% vs 32.0%, -5.3pp) and product completion (20.8% vs 17.0%, -3.8pp). Key findings:

• Option 1 drives higher FS concentration — 71.8% of Opt 1 completers file as Full Service vs 55.3% for Opt 2. The 16.5pp FS mix gap has significant revenue implications
• Opt 2 shifts volume to lower-value SKUs — DIY is 23.5% of Opt 2 completers vs 11.5% for Opt 1; DIWM is 19.4% vs 13.5%
• Concierge paths strongly favor Opt 1 — 61.0% of Opt 1 goes through concierge vs 37.0% for Opt 2, with combined FS rate of 34.3% vs 26.1%
• Opt 2 PS transfer captures ~30% of traffic — but yields only 1.0% FS completion. The revenue opportunity cost of rerouting to PS remains substantial
• Agent Missed outcome is a key differentiator — Opt 1 achieves 39.9% completion vs 29.3% for Opt 2, a 10.6pp gap
• Recommendation: Option 1 is the higher-performing prompt — it drives more appointments through concierge, achieves higher FS conversion, and yields a significantly richer SKU mix
