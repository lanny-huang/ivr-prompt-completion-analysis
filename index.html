12. The “Right Prompt” Analysis — Are Customers Selecting Correctly?

Opt 1 callers are well-matched: 62.9% already in FS, 89% state FS preference, 39% had assisted filing last year, 81% are New customers needing guidance. The ~23% starting in DIY/DIWM are “convertible” — they’re trying DIY but pressing 1 signals they want help, and 35-54% end up in FS. These are high-value upsell opportunities the concierge path captures well.

Opt 2 has a significant misrouting problem: ~50% of Opt 2 callers are already in FS AND 79% state FS as their filing preference. They want expert help but pressed 2 (“tech support / already filed”), likely because the prompt wording was ambiguous. The PS transfer path they get routed to yields only 1% FS completion vs 34% for the concierge path. This misrouting is the single largest driver of the performance gap between prompts.

The ~12% of Opt 2 who state DIY preference + 33% starting in DIY/DIWM are likely the correctly-routed tech-support seekers. They’re more self-sufficient (64% filed themselves last year) and appropriately seeking help with a technical issue rather than expert filing.

13. Summary & Recommendation

Bottom line (full season data): With the complete data window, Option 1 meaningfully outperforms Option 2 on both appointment handling (37.2% vs 32.0%, -5.3pp) and product completion (20.8% vs 17.0%, -3.8pp). Key findings:

• Option 1 drives higher FS concentration — 71.8% of Opt 1 completers file as Full Service vs 55.3% for Opt 2. The 16.5pp FS mix gap has significant revenue implications
• Opt 2 shifts volume to lower-value SKUs — DIY is 23.5% of Opt 2 completers vs 11.5% for Opt 1; DIWM is 19.4% vs 13.5%
• Concierge paths strongly favor Opt 1 — 61.0% of Opt 1 goes through concierge vs 37.0% for Opt 2, with combined FS rate of 34.3% vs 26.1%
• Opt 2 PS transfer captures ~30% of traffic — but yields only 1.0% FS completion. The revenue opportunity cost of rerouting to PS remains substantial
• Agent Missed outcome is a key differentiator — Opt 1 achieves 39.9% completion vs 29.3% for Opt 2, a 10.6pp gap
• Opt 1 is the FS conversion engine — non-FS starters convert to FS at 15-22pp higher rates via concierge vs PS transfer (e.g. DIY Paid: 37.5% vs 16.0% → FS)
• ~50% of Opt 2 callers are likely misrouted — they’re already in FS and state FS preference, but pressed 2 for “tech support.” Clearer prompt wording could recapture this volume
• VOC themes confirm friction drivers — technical failures, service delivery issues, and appointment availability are the top reasons callers don’t convert
• Recommendation: Option 1 is the higher-performing prompt — it drives more appointments through concierge, achieves higher FS conversion, and yields a significantly richer SKU mix. Consider refining Prompt 2 wording to reduce misrouting of FS-intent callers
